Who I Am

My name is Zaccaria Scarpa. I'm a second-year Statistics major at Florida State University, originally from Seattle, Washington. I spend most of my time split between school and work. Specifically working as a full-stack developer at Cofeature, a startup where we're building an AI-powered marketing agent for local service businesses.

Outside of academics and work, I am greatly involved in my fraternity on campus, having served as Communications Director and now the Provost for the Zeta Alpha chapter of Zeta Beta Tau at FSU, and I have a great interest in cooking as well as Seattle sports, especially the Seahawks and Mariners, which have always been a constant in my life.

This ePortfolio

This site was built for ENC 3416: Digital Writing. It collects three pieces of writing produced throughout the semester, each in a different genre and medium. A journalism-style magazine spread, a nonfiction newspaper interview, and a key terms concept map. Along with a reflective essay connecting them through the lens of intertextuality from our course readings.

The goal isn't just to display finished work. As I argue in my reflection, the ePortfolio is itself a rhetorical act. An exhibit of who I am as a writer and thinker, shaped by the audiences and discourse communities I've learned to navigate.
